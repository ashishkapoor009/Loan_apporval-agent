Loan Approval Policy (Sample Automated Decision Policy) 
1. Purpose 
This policy defines a standardized framework for evaluating incoming loan applications using 
automated decision rules based on: 
• Applicant credit score  
• Verified income / repayment affordability  
• Requested loan amount  
The objective is to consistently recommend whether an application should be: 
• Approved  
• Rejected  
• Flagged for Manual Review  
This is a sample policy for decision automation and should be adapted to the lender’s regulatory, 
risk, and business requirements. 
 
2. Scope 
This policy applies to all retail unsecured loan applications submitted through digital or assisted 
channels. 
This policy does not replace: 
• KYC verification  
• Fraud screening  
• Document verification  
• Regulatory compliance checks  
• Sanctions / AML checks  
• Final underwriter discretion  
 
3. Input Parameters 
3.1 Credit Score 
Credit score is used as an indicator of repayment history and creditworthiness. 
Accepted source: 
• Bureau credit report / credit scoring agency  
Required field: 
• Numeric credit score  
 
3.2 Verified Income 
Applicant income must be verified through approved documentation. 
Accepted evidence: 
• Salary slips  
• Bank statements  
• Income tax returns  
• Employer verification  
• Business financials (for self-employed applicants)  
Required field: 
• Monthly verified income  
 
3.3 Requested Loan Amount 
The total loan amount requested by the applicant. 
Required field: 
• Requested principal amount

5. Decision Logic 
5.1 Approve 
Application may be automatically approved when ALL of the following are true: 
• Credit score ≥ 750  
• EMI burden ≤ 35% of verified monthly income  
• Requested loan amount within acceptable threshold  
• Income successfully verified  
• No fraud indicators detected  
• KYC completed successfully  
• Compliance checks passed  
System Decision: APPROVE 
 
5.2 Reject 
Application may be automatically rejected when ANY of the following conditions are met: 
• Credit score < 650  
• EMI burden > 50%  
• Income unverifiable  
• Requested loan amount significantly exceeds repayment capacity  
• Fraud indicators detected  
• Material inconsistencies in application data  
• Failed KYC / compliance screening  
System Decision: REJECT 
 
5.3 Manual Review 
Application should be routed for human review when: 
• Credit score falls between 650–749  
• EMI burden is between 35%–50%  
• Borderline affordability exists  
• Exceptional high-value customer case  
• Conflicting applicant information  
• Non-standard income structure  
• High but explainable loan requirement  
• Partial documentation issues  
• Fraud suspicion requires analyst validation  
System Decision: MANUAL REVIEW

7. Fraud & Compliance Controls 
Mandatory controls before final approval: 
• Identity verification  
• Address verification  
• PAN / tax validation  
• AML screening  
• Sanctions screening  
• Fraud device fingerprinting  
• Duplicate application detection  
• Document authenticity checks  
Failure in mandatory control checks may override automated approval. 
 
8. Exception Handling 
Exceptions may be permitted only through authorized approval where: 
• High net worth customer relationship exists  
• Additional collateral is available  
• Strong compensating factors exist  
• Verified exceptional income sources exist  
All exceptions must include: 
• Reason code  
• Approver identity  
• Timestamp  
• Audit trail entry  
 
9. Governance 
Policy owner: 
Credit Risk / Underwriting Team 
Review frequency: 
Quarterly or as business conditions require 
Triggers for policy review: 
• Rising default rates  
• Economic stress  
• Regulatory change  
• Portfolio performance deterioration  
• Fraud trend changes  
 
10. Audit Requirements 
The system must retain: 
• Application inputs  
• Decision outputs  
• Rule triggered  
• Override reason  
• Reviewer notes  
• Timestamp  
• User / system identity  
Retention should align with regulatory requirements. 
 
11. Disclaimer 
This policy provides recommendation logic for automated decision support. 
Final lending approval remains subject to: 
• Institution credit policy  
• Regulatory obligations  
• Human oversight where required  
• Risk appetite controls
